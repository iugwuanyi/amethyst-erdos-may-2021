SCHEDULE 1

Project Description

The objective of the Project is to increase the utilization and quality of primary
health care services, with an emphasis on the detection and management of non-
communicable diseases in high-risk population groups, in the Selected Areas of the
Borrower.

The Project consists of the following parts:
Part 1: Implementation of the PHC System Reorganization and Strengthening Strategies

(a) Provision of overall strategic focus for the PHC reorganization as well as its detailed
policies, standards and implementation guidelines.

(b) Support to strengthen PMCI capabilities and services for more comprehensive and
quality care.

(c) Support to personal health record system and improvement of the national procurement
and supply chain system.

(d) Development and implementation of a community engagement mechanisms.

(e) Support for utilization of PHC services, with a focus on detection and management of
non-communicable diseases.

Part 2: Project Implementation Support and Innovation Grants
(a) Support to Project management, coordination and monitoring.

(b) Support to the results verification and operational research to assess the health needs,
demand, utilization and quality of basic health service delivery.

(c) Provision of training and technical assistance to the MoH and Provinces to build
strategic, managerial and technical capacity for the health sector.

(d) Provision of the Competitive Small Grants to support innovations in priority areas,
including but not limited to: (i) patient-friendly service delivery improvements;
(ii) technological innovations in basic health service delivery; (iii) outreach and
empowerment of high-risk groups; (iv) civil engagement; and (v) public-private
partnerships.

SCHEDULE 2

Project Execution

Section 1. Implementation Arrangements

Institutional Arrangements

The Borrower shall maintain, throughout the implementation of the Project, the
Project Management Unit, with adequate professional and administrative staff in
numbers and with qualification and experience and under terms of reference
satisfactory to the Bank, and headed by a Project director, which unit shall, inter
alia: (a) carry out day-to-day overall Project coordination including overall
financial management and reporting; (b) carry out quality control as well as
facilitate and coordinate procurement activities; (c) monitor and evaluate the
Project’s performance; and (d) manage the Project-level grievance and complaints
procedures acceptable to the Bank.

The Borrower shall establish, not later than three (3) months after the Effective
Date, and thereafter maintain, until the completion of the Project, a National
Steering Committee to monitor the implementation progress and provide overall
oversight and guidance.

Implementation Arrangements

To facilitate the carrying out of the Project at the provincial level, the Borrower,
through its MPCLGS in consultation with its MoH, shall ensure that:

(a) each PC shall carry out the Project in accordance with the Anti-Corruption
Guidelines;

(b) each PC shall report to the MPCLGS and MoH in a timely manner any
issues impacting or potentially impacting the implementation of the
requirements of this Section; and

(c) each PC shall allocate sufficient and appropriately qualified staff with
adequate resources to enable the PC to carry out the Project.

The Borrower shall: (a) ensure that all budgetary allocations related to the Project
are released, in a timely manner, to the appropriate agencies of the Borrower and
of the PCs, as shall be required for the effective implementation of the Project; and
(b) apply suitable internal controls to ensure that payments of budgetary
expenditures, including monthly or quarterly reconciliations thereof, shall be made
in a timely manner.
Competitive Small Grants

The Borrower, through Project Management Unit, shall: (a) carry out the
Competitive Small Grants in accordance with the Operational Guidelines; and (b)
not amend, revise or waive, nor allow to be amended, revised or waived the
provisions of the Operational Guidelines or any part thereof without the prior
written agreement of the Bank.

In the event of any inconsistencies between the Operational Guidelines and this
Agreement, the provisions of this Agreement shall prevail.

Annual Work Plans and Budgets

The Borrower shall furnish to the Bank for approval as soon as available, but in
any case, not later than November 30 of each year during Project implementation,
a proposed annual work plan and budget for Part 2 of the Project and the updated
Procurement Plan for each subsequent fiscal year, of such scope and detail as the
Bank shall have reasonably requested, except for the annual work plan and budget
for the first Fiscal Year which shall be furnished prior to the commencement of the
relevant activities under the Project.

The Borrower shall, not later than two (2) months after furnishing each annual
work plan and budget referred to in the preceding paragraph to the Bank, finalize
and adopt, and thereafter ensure that the Project is carried out in accordance with,
such plan and budget as agreed in writing with the Bank.

APPENDIX

Definitions

“Anti-Corruption Guidelines” means, for purposes of paragraph 5 of the Appendix to the
General Conditions, the “Guidelines on Preventing and Combating Fraud and Corruption
in Projects Financed by IBRD Loans and IDA Credits and Grants”, dated October 15, 2006
and revised in January 2011 and as of July 1, 2016.

“Category” means a category set forth in the table in Section II.A of Schedule 2 to this
Agreement.

“Competitive Small Grant” means a grant made or proposed to be made by the Borrower
under Part 2(d) of the Project, pursuant to the provision of this Agreement and the
Operational Guidelines.

“Contingent Emergency Response Component Manual” or the acronym “CERCM” means
the plan referred to in Section L.E of Schedule 2 to this Agreement, to be adopted by the
Borrower for the Contingent Emergency Response Part in accordance with the provisions
of said Section.

"Contingent Emergency Response Part" means Part 3 of the Project.

“Disbursement Linked Indicator” or the acronym “DLI” means an indicator set forth in the
table in Schedule 4 to this Agreement, on the basis of which a DLR is to be measured; and
“Disbursement Linked Indicators” or “DLIs” means, collectively, more than one, or all
said indicators (as the context may require).

“Disbursement Linked Result” or the acronym “DLR” means a result, referred to in the
table in Schedule 4 to this Agreement; and “Disbursement Linked Results” or “DLRs”
means, collectively, more than one, or all said results (as the context may require).

“DLR Period” means the Fiscal Year specified in the table in Schedule 4 to this Agreement,
during which a DLR is set to be achieved.

“DLR Value” means an amount of the Credit allocated to each DLR and set forth in the
table in Schedule 4 to this Agreement, which may be withdrawn by the Borrower following
the achievement of the respective DLR in accordance with the requirements of this
Agreement.

“Eligible Crisis or Emergency” means an event that has caused, or is likely to imminently
cause, a major adverse economic and/or social impact to the Borrower, associated with a
natural or man-made crisis or disaster.

“Eligible Expenditure Program” or the acronym “EEP” means the eligible expenditure
program under Part 1 of the Project, whose budget or general ledger code numbers for such
expenditure items as the following may be changed from time to time: salaries and wages
(1001) under the Provincial Departments of Health Services under the PCs.

“Emergency Expenditure” means any of the eligible expenditures set forth in the
Contingency Emergency Response Component Manual in accordance with the provisions
of Section L.E of Schedule 2 to this Agreement, and required for the Contingent Emergency
Response Part.

“Fiscal Year” or “FY” means the Borrower’s fiscal year, commencing on January 1 of each
calendar year and concluding on December 31 of the same calendar year.

“General Conditions” means the “International Bank for Reconstruction and Development
General Conditions for IBRD Financing, Investment Project Financing”, dated July 14,
2017.

“Incremental Operating Costs” means the incremental expenditures incurred on account of
the management of Part B of the Project, including for per diems, office supplies, medical
supplies/drugs, vehicle rental charges (including passenger insurance), government
motorcycle and vehicle operating charges (fuel, maintenance, and insurance), utilities,
maintenance of office equipment, telephone and other communications charges, office rent,
advertising costs for procuring goods, works and services, bank charges for the operation
of the designated account, salaries of contractual staff, salary top-up and Project related
allowances of officials of the Borrower’s civil and public service, but excluding their
salaries, in accordance with the Management Services Department of the Borrower’s
Ministry of Finance circular #01/2016, or any successor thereto.

“MoH” means the Borrower’s Ministry of Health, Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine, or
any successor thereto.

“MPCLGS” means the Borrower’s Ministry of Provincial Councils, Local Governments
and Sports, or any successor thereto.

“MSD” means the Medical Supply Division of the MoH of the Borrower.

“National Steering Committee” means the committee to be established pursuant to Section
I.A.2 of Schedule 2 to this Agreement, co-chaired by the Secretaries of the MoH and
MPCLGS.

“NCD” means the non-communicable disease.

“Operational Guidelines” means the guidelines dated May 16, 2018, setting forth the
procedures and eligible criteria for Competitive Small Grants under Part 2(d) of the Project.

“PC” means the Provincial Council, a body having jurisdiction over each of the Provinces
as provided for in the 13" amendment to the Constitution of the Borrower.

“PHC” means a primary health center.

“PMCI” means a primary medical care institution.

“Preparation Advance” means the portion of the advance referred to in Section 2.07 (a) of

agreement signed on behalf of the Bank on January 26, 2018 and on behalf of the Borrower
on January 30, 2018.

“Procurement Regulations” means, for purposes of paragraph 85 of the Appendix to the
General Conditions, the “World Bank Procurement Regulations for IPF Borrowers”, dated
July 2016, revised November 2017.

“Project Management Unit” means the project management unit established on March 31,
2018 for the purpose of implementing the Project and referred to in Section I.A.1 of
Schedule 2 to this Agreement.

“Province” means any of the provinces in the territory of the Borrower as provided in the
13" amendment to the Constitution of the Borrower.

“Provincial Departments of Health Services” means the administrative unit within each
provincial level, responsible for the management of health services.

“Selected Areas” means the empanelment areas of PMCIs which are selected by the
Borrower.

“Signature Date” means the later of the two dates on which the Borrower and the Bank
signed this Agreement and such definition applies to all references to “the date of the Loan
Agreement” in the General Conditions.

“Training” means the costs of training activities under Part B of the Project, based on
annual work plans and budgets approved by the Bank, and attributable to seminars,
workshops, and domestic and overseas study tours, along with travel and subsistence
allowances for training participants, services of trainers, rental of training facilities,
preparation and reproduction of training materials, and other activities directly related to
course preparation and implementation.

“Verification Report” means each report to be provided by the Borrower under terms of
reference satisfactory to the Bank as evidence that one or more DLRs has been met as
required by Section II1.B.1(b) of Schedule 2 to this Agreement.
