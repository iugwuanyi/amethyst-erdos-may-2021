SCHEDULE 2
Description of the Project

The objectives of the Project are: (a) to improve the safety and soundness of the
financial system through improved prudential regulation and supervision, and through
greater incentives for self-policing by market participants; (b) to support the development
of the pension system; and (c) to improve public investment evaluation and budgeting.

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof
as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such
objectives:

Part A: Financial Sector Regulation

Provision of coordinated techﬁical assistance through SHCP with respect to:
1. CNBV

(a) Banks and Financial Groups:

Analysis and improvement of: authorization policies and procedures;
regulatory and supervision policies; regulatory accounting principles; supervision processes
(both jn situ and extra situ) and consolidated supervision of financial conglomerates;
modernization of the information systems; and institutionalization of human resource
.development.

(b) Securities Markets and Brokerage Firms:

Analysis and improvement of: the regulatory and  supervisory policy
framework of the organized securities markets and brokerage firms; self-regulation; and
the legal, regulatory and supervision policy framework for new financial instruments such
as, for example, derivatives.

(c) Support for the execution of the Program:

Support for the execution of the Program including, inter alia, technical
assistance with respect to the Guarantor’s accelerated program of bank inspections,
diagnostic studies of banks, selection of advisors for bank restructurings, a study of
appraisal standards, review of impediments to securitization of bank and non-bank
originated assets, the UDI-scheme, a study of the legal framework for secured
transactions, and a study of the obstacles to voluntary debt restructuring (including
recognition, execution and enforcement of out-of-court workouts and arbitration awards).
CNSF

Analysis and lmprovement of: supemslon processes; regulatory and superVnsory
policies applicable to insurance companies, insurance brokers, adjusters and reinsurance
companies; CNSF’s capacity to evaluate actuarial audits; certification procedures for
actuaries; and regulatory reporting standards and accounting principles consistent with
those adopted in the other Commissions. ‘

3. CONSAR
(a) Regulatorv Framework:

The establishment of a regulatory framework for the private investment
management of SAR accounts, the development of a consistent regulatory framework for
voluntary occupational retirement plans offered by private sector and parastatal
enterorises, the development of CONSAR’s capacity to supervise and inspect the SAR
accounts, the design of a public communications program in support of pension reform,
and preparation of a human resource development program.

(b) Annuities Market Study:
Execution of a study by CONSAR, jointly with CNSF, of the annuities

market and its implications for its further development as the SAR matures, with emphasis
on international experience.

Part B: Public Investment Evaluation and Budgeting

Provision of technical assistance to SHCP’s unit responsible for public investment
evaluation and budgeting with respect to: inter-institutional coordination; establishment
of evaluation standards, dissemination of these standards and training in project
evaluation; evaluation of sector- and region-specific public investment projects;
establishment of a national inventory of public investment projects and a system for
ranking and prioritization within and across economic sectors; suiti-annual budgeting; and
ex-post monitoring and evaluation.
